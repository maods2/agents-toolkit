# Scientific Writing

## Purpose

Communicate research clearly with claims supported by evidence, methods, and limitations.

## When to use

Use this skill for scientific papers, technical reports, experiment summaries, related work, methods, results, discussion, or reviewer-response preparation.

## Decision rules

- Separate claims from evidence.
- State the research question and contribution early.
- Describe methods with enough detail for reproduction.
- Report limitations and threats to validity.
- Keep terminology consistent.

## Anti-patterns

- Overclaiming beyond the evidence.
- Hiding negative or inconclusive results.
- Mixing background, method, and results without structure.
- Citing work without explaining relevance.

## Checklist

- Define audience and venue.
- State contribution.
- Link claims to results or citations.
- Check method reproducibility.
- Add limitations and future work.
